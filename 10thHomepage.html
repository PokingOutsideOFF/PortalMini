The Stream of Science

Why Science Stream?

The science stream mainly consists of scientific theories and its practical concepts. It helps students to understand the concepts of physics, chemistry and biology with mathematics as its tool which further helps them to develop analytical and problem-solving skills to understand and conquer real time problems of the world.

It has a broad list of career opportunities, Space Exploration, Data Science, Software Developer, Pharmaceuticals to name a few.

If man-made marvels, technology, or outer space enchants you or discovering new elements, new medicines and many more things fascinates you then taking science stream would be a smart option.

Structure of the Stream

It is mostly divided into two sub-streams: PCM (non-medical) and PCM-B (medical).

Both the streams have physics, chemistry and a language (most probably English) in common. The option between Mathematics and Biology makes the two sub-streams different. Biology is generally preferred by those who want to purse their career in medical field, while mathematics is opted by those who generally want to start their career like but not limited to engineering, pilot, statistician, architecture, analyst.

Along with the above mandatory subjects, students have to choose an elective subject. Computer Science, Economics, Home Science, Physical Education, Psychology are popular electives.

  • Physics

    Focuses on understanding the functioning of our surroundings. In Class 11- 12 it provides an introduction to topics of electromagnetism, thermodynamics, optics, mechanics, nuclear physics and solid and liquid state physics.

  • Chemistry

    Focuses on study of composition of matter, structure of the matter and various changes happening with chemical reactions. In Class 11-12 the subject dives deep into concepts of chemical bonding, kinetics, atomic and molecular structures while also providing an introduction to organic chemistry, metallurgy and synthetic materials.

  • Mathematics

    Focuses on solving many day-to-day problems and acts as a tool to mostly every subject. In Class 11-12 it provides a base for understanding the broad concept of calculus, algebra, vectors, linear programming, 3D geometry and probability.

  • Biology

    Focuses on studying living organisms, their living mechanisms, chemical processes and evolution. In Class 11-12 it provides in-depth understanding of human and plant physiology, genetics, evolution and provides a connection with real-life technology, environment, agriculture and industry.

The Stream of Commerce

Why Commerce stream?

The commerce stream includes working with financial models and the working of economies. It involves working with numbers and quick calculations.

So if you like working with numbers or need help with starting a business, this stream can be the right choice for you.

Structure of the stream

The core subjects in Commerce are: Accountancy, Economics and Business Studies. A language subject is also compulsory in the course, and a choice of Maths against some other subject like Informatics Practices, etc. After 10th commerce students have career options like Chartered Accountancy, Banking & Insurance, Finance, Stockbroking, Financial Planning, and much more.

  • Accountancy

    Accountancy or accounting is the process of recording financial transactions pertaining to a company. The information found is consolidated into reports which in monetary terms are known as financial statements. This subject introduces this concept of maintaining financial records by following particular conventions and rules pertaining to these records.

  • Economics

    This subject focuses on how wealth is manufactured and distributed. It has a theoretical component as well as a practical component to it. Product and service creation/manufacturing and buying and selling is the area of learning which is provided. This has applications not only in the field of economics but other fields like health and law. This subject is a social science, meaning it has a relation with studying people and how their relationship with any product or service affects it.

  • Business Studies

    This is an educational subject which focuses on providing the academic knowledge and necessary training to plan, execute, supervise and analyse any business enterprise. All the functional departments like financial, Human Resources and more is taught in this subject which will provide the knowledge to establish a business which can generate revenue.

The Stream of Arts/Humanities

Why Arts/Humanities?

Careers options in this stream involve creativeness and strong comunication skills. This stream is considered to be unconventional or not any parent's first choice. But the career scope after pursuing this is quite diverse, exciting and interesting. So, if you are creative, have a strong artistic/literary side or interested in academic research, this stream can be the right choice for you.

Structure of the stream

The core subjects offered in Arts are Sociology, History, Literature, Political Science, Psychology, Economics, Philosophy, Fine Arts, etc along with one compulsory language subject.

  • Sociology

    Sociology is a social science which conducts the study of social life, social change and the social causes and consequences of human behavior. Sociologists investigate the structure of groups, organizations and societies, and how people interact within these contexts. In simple words, it is the study of social behaviour of humans and how it affects themselves and the people around them. Social research has influence throughout various industries and sectors of life, such as among politicians, policy makers and legislators, educators, planners, administrators, developers, business magnates and managers, social workers, non-governmental organizations and non-profit organizations, as well as individuals interested in resolving social issues in general. As such, there is often a great deal of overlap between social research, market research, and other statistical fields.

  • Psychology

    Psychology is the scientific study of the mind and behavior. Psychologists are actively involved in studying and understanding mental processes, brain functions and behavior. It is a subject which helps to link actions and behaviour to neuroscience. While it's affiliation with the healthcare sector is known, it can also be directed towards solving problems in other spheres of human activity. Research can also be conducted on a large scale which will ultimately prove beneficial for people.

  • Fine Arts

    This is a subject which explores the art world not for it's functionality but for it's asthetic and beauty. Fine art is rooted in drawing and design-based works such as painting, printmaking, and sculpture.

Vocational Stream

Many boards offer vocational subjects after class 10. These subjects prepare you for a job immediately after the completion of class 12. Depending upon the subjects offered by your school, your vocational subject choices could be Accountancy and Taxation, Auto Shop Repair and Practice, Business Operations and Administration, Capital Market Operations, Civil Engineering Technician, Food Nutrition and Dietetics, Food Production, Hospitality Management, Music Production, Textile Design, Web Applications, etc. These subjects undoubtedly enhance your scope of what is next after 10th.
The ITI’s, Polytechnic Diplomas, Paramedical and Vocational courses discussed below, make you job-ready and make you part of the manpower in the industries you would work in. They offer technical job oriented options that can help you seek employment soon after completing class 10. Top ITI Courses are Computer Hardware & Network Maintenance, Draughtsman (Civil), Mechanic (Auto Electricals & Electronics), Interior Decoration and Designing, Computer Operator and Programming Assistant, Baker and Confectioner, Sheet Metal, Plumbing, etc.

Polytechnic course

They are cost-effective diploma courses which can be joined after class 10 or class 12. You can opt for courses in Mechanical, Civil, Chemical, Computer, Marine Technology, Textile Technology, Automobile, etc. The Polytechnic colleges offer diploma courses for 3 years, 2 years, 1 year. The 3-year diploma course in Engineering gets you a lateral entry in the 2nd year of B.E/B.Tech. For students who do not wish to continue the regular school study pattern, this could be one of the options for what is next after 10th class.

Paramedic course

The Paramedical branch is allied to the healthcare sector and offers good opportunities. What is next after 10th for students who take up Paramedical courses are options of becoming an X-Ray Technician, Dialysis Technician, Nursing Assistance, Ophthalmic Technician, etc.

Apart from focusing on choosing the right stream and subjects, it is also essential to keep working on yourself and building on skills that will prepare you for the immense competition in the job market. The more aware we are of what is happening around the world, the more we become ready to enter the industry. It is always beneficial to start early.

  • Start working on your soft skills like communication skills, interpersonal skills, and so on.
  • Learn a new language.
  • Develop a habit of reading.
